Ground Support Equipment (GSE)

GSE does not fly. It never will. But without it, nothing on the spacecraft would ever get tested. GSE is your daily working environment as an ATLO engineer.

What GSE Is

GSE falls into two categories. Electrical GSE (EGSE) -- equipment used to power, command, and monitor the spacecraft electrically. Mechanical GSE (MGSE) -- equipment used to physically handle, position, and transport spacecraft hardware.

GSE on Flatsat vs. Flight Vehicle

On flatsat, GSE is the primary interface -- filling gaps where flight harnesses or power systems are not yet fully installed. At the flight vehicle, GSE shifts to a support role, but the discipline of using it correctly does not change. A wrong setting on an external power supply can damage flight hardware just as easily as engineering hardware.

KEY POINT

GSE is configuration-controlled. Every item -- including every test rack -- is documented: what it is, what configuration it is in, when it was last calibrated. Out-of-calibration GSE cannot be used on the vehicle.
