Why Connector Knowledge Matters

Mating and demating connectors is one of the most frequent physical tasks an ATLO engineer performs. Getting it wrong -- forcing a connector, mismatching pins, or applying incorrect torque -- can bend pins, damage sockets, or create an intermittent connection that causes an anomaly much later in the campaign.

Mate/Demate Discipline

Before mating any connector, verify you have the correct mating pair -- connector keying prevents most mismatches, but not all. Align connectors straight, never at an angle. Never force a connector. If it does not mate smoothly, stop and investigate before applying more force.

KEY POINT

CSTM and ISTM checks exist specifically because connector mating is a point where mistakes are common and consequences are real. Verify before you mate. Every time.
